K-town Food and Culture

Throughout the years, food in Korea Town has flourished and adapted from other types of food due to its diverse population which has brought about the creation of fusion foods.These include, Korean-Colombian, Korean-Mexican, Korean American, and other types of fusions, all derived from Korean food and a different type of food. Food has grown not only to satisfy our culinary needs, but also the need to bring people and families to a place where they can relax and be with one another.

Food culture has also allowed people to bond with one another over food. Food Culture plays a big role in out current society due to the fact that social media has been a place where people can share their creations and differen types of fusion food. It highlights places such as KoreaTown because of its diversity in food.

Gentrification and the effects on Ktowns Culture

This year on February 9th, The los Angeles city council approved the K-town project, this project is supposed to bring about the creation of 52+ new buildings. These buildings will be finished by 2020 if all goes according to plan. The Korea town project may sound like a good thing to most people but this will actually increase prices by the hundreds and maybe even thousands, not only that but it will kick our communties out of their homes. This will also bring the loss of the culture and the loss of our food which we have taken so long to develop in our homes here in K-town.
Near my home and most likely near yours there are many different buildings that are being planned for demolition and leasing. Your favorite mom and pops resturaunt will be be turned into another Mc.Donalds and your friends home will be turned into a galleria or another big corporate store. Soon enough even you will be forced to evict your home and move into a different neighborhood and lose everything that you had worked so hard to build.
